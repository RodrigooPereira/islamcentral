How to Prepare for Ramadan

Ramadan, the holiest month of the Islamic lunar calendar, is a time of reflection, self-restraint, and spiritual growth for Muslims around the world. It is a month when believers fast from dawn to sunset, abstaining from food and drink, as well as engaging in acts of worship and self-reflection. As the month of Ramadan approaches, it is important to prepare both physically and mentally to make the most of this sacred time. Insha’Allah in this article, we will discuss how to effectively prepare for Ramadan, ensuring a meaningful and fulfilling experience.

First and foremost, it is essential to understand the significance and purpose of fasting during Ramadan. Fasting is not merely about abstaining from food and water, it is a means of attaining self-discipline, empathy, and spiritual connection with Allah. Prior to the start of Ramadan, take the time to educate yourself about the various aspects of fasting and its rewards. Read books, listen to lectures, or seek guidance from knowledgeable individuals to deepen your understanding and motivation.

1. Fast Voluntarily

The best way to prepare fasting for a whole month is to start fasting often, in order to gain control over your actions, stomach and desires.

Usamah ibn Zayd (may Allah be pleased with him) said: I said, "Oh Messenger of Allah, I do not see you fasting in any other month like you fast in Shabaan." He said, "That is a month to which people do not pay attention, between Rajab and Ramadan, and it is a month in which deeds are lifted up to the Lord of the Worlds. I like for my deeds to be lifted up when I am fasting."” (Narrated by al-Nasaai)

2. Read and Reflect Upon the Quran

One of the central pillars of Ramadan is the increased devotion to the Quran. The Quran has underlying meanings and stories which teaches us the way of life.

Aishah (may Allah be pleased with her) related that the Prophet ﷺ said: “Indeed the one who recites the Quran beautifully, smoothly and precisely, will be in the company of the noble and obedient angels. As for the one who recites with difficulty, stammering or stumbling through its verses, then he will have twice that reward.” (Muslim)

3. Improve Your Character

Ramadan serves as a unique opportunity for self-reflection and personal growth, particularly in the refinement of one's character.

The Prophet ﷺ used to say: “The best amongst you are those who have the best manners and character.” (al-Bukhari)

4. Eat Healthy and Moderately

Eating healthily is crucial for maintaining physical well-being, especially during Ramadan when the body experiences changes due to fasting. As you embark on the month of Ramadan, where fasting is a central practice, it's important to pay attention to the quality and nutritional value of the foods you consume.

The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: "A human being fills no worse vessel than his stomach. It is sufficient for a human being to eat a few mouthfuls to keep his spine straight. But if he must (fill it), then one third of food, one third for drink and one third for air."”

Ash-Shafi’i said: “”I have not filled myself in 16 years because filling oneself makes the body heavy, removes clean understanding, induces sleep and makes one weak for worship.”

The Prophet ﷺ said, “A believer eats in one intestine, and a kafir (unbeliever) or a hypocrite eats in seven intestines." (Bukhaari)

5. Engage with the Community

Community engagement is a significant aspect of Ramadan, emphasizing the importance of connection, support, and collaboration within the Muslim community.

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said, “The believers in their mutual kindness, compassion, and sympathy are like one body; when one part of it suffers, the whole body responds with sleeplessness and fever” (Sahih Muslim).

6. Exercise Regularly

Engaging in regular exercise during Ramadan is not only permissible but also highly encouraged as it contributes to overall health, helps maintain energy levels, and supports mental well-being.

Abu Hurayrah (may Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said: “The strong believer is better and more beloved to Allah than the weak believer, although both are good.”

7. Spiritual Preparation

As the month of Ramadan approaches, Muslims are encouraged to intensify their prayers, seeking a deeper connection with Allah and striving for spiritual growth.

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said: "No Salat is more burdensome to the hypocrites than the Fajr (dawn) prayer and the 'Isha' (night) prayer; and if they knew their merits, they would come to them even if they had to crawl to do so." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim].

"O you who have believed, seek help through patience and prayer. Indeed, Allah is with the patient."" (Quran, 2:153)

"They were succeeded by a people who neglected the Prayers and pursued their lusts. They shall presently meet with their doom" (Quran 19:59)

Ramadan presents a unique opportunity for holistic self-improvement, encompassing spiritual, physical, and communal dimensions. By incorporating practices such as increased prayer, thoughtful reflection on the Quran, healthier eating habits, and active community engagement, you embark on a transformative journey. These intentional efforts not only enhance your connection with Allah but also contribute to personal growth, improved character, and a strengthened sense of community. As you embrace the spirit of Ramadan, may your endeavors lead to lasting positive changes, fostering a more profound sense of gratitude, compassion, and spiritual fulfillment in your life. Ramadan Mubarak!
